Movie Reviews

March 2022 Edition: Movies to Watch

John N. Gagne

March 2022

Photographer: Michelle Scarn

THE BATMAN (2022) ★★★★

The Batman feels new. It's a refreshing and exciting take on a story as old as film itself. It's grimy, it's dark, and, most importantly, it's down to earth. To be honest, it feels like a big step towards making better superhero movies. Matt Reeves took the project by the horns and did it his way, which is apparent throughout the film. Whenever directors of superhero movies take charge of their productions, the films are always better. Batman feels independent, leaps and bounds above the rest. Think Thor: Ragnarok or Guardians of the Galaxy. It has a unique take on a lot of original Batman characters, portraying their lives as actual citizens as well as their super-powered alter egos. Plus, the music fits the vibe of this darker bat immaculately, as if they made the movie after choosing the soundtrack. The Batman is definitely a must-watch if you're going to the theatre. And yes... it's better than The Dark Knight.

LICORICE PIZZA (2022) ★★★★

Licorice Pizza is pure charm. It’s a feel-good movie that contrasts adulthood with the innocence of high school and the yearning to be an adult. We follow two protagonists: 25-year-old Alana and 15-year-old entrepreneur Gary, watching as Gary falls for Alana and the prospective liberty of adulthood. It’s an extremely funny and lighthearted watch; though long, it goes by frighteningly fast, leaving you wanting to see the rest of the characters' lives. From the protagonists to the extras to the salesman selling cars on a billboard, every character feels genuine. Like these were all individuals that had passed through the director's life. They feel more like memories than characters. Like every last one has a story to tell that we just happened to stumble upon. And this realness doesn’t come from any rigid plotline. Instead, the film is a loose story told throughout several impactful moments in the characters' lives, thus making it feel exponentially more personal. Licorice Pizza is pure charm.

MILLER'S CROSSING (1990) ★★★★★

From legendary writer-director duo of Joel and Ethan Coen comes Miller’s Crossing. A revenge story set in mob-riddled, Prohibition-era America, about what happens when you count out the wrong guy. It’s fast, it’s funny, it’s star-studded, and it’s beautiful to look at. Miller's Crossing is, in my opinion, the best of the Coen Brothers movies. They really hit their stride and show just what they can do when given the freedom. The writing in and of itself is fantastic; it takes a real pro to be able to write lines that are so good that even the most juvenile of actors can recite them with success. The dialogue really transcends the movie itself.

There is also the comedy. The Coen brothers are known for having more than funny movies, but Miller’s Crossing is on a level of its own. It’s a comedy without being a comedy. It’s dark, and yet you can’t help but laugh. Throughout the film, you get the sense that the Coens were laughing just as hard while writing it as you are while watching it. Finally, the cherry on top has to be the performances. John Turturro gives the performance of his career, Marcia Gay Harden expertly sells being a leading love interest, and Frances McDormand absolutely stuns in her fifteen seconds of screen time. Miller’s Crossing gets an A+ in every category and yet remains one of the Coen brothers' least talked about movies. Do yourself a favour and watch it.
